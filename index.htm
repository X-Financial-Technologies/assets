1 INTRODUCTION

USDX is a yield-bearing stablecoin backed 1:1 by municipal bonds. XFT structured USDX as a closed-end tender fund under the Investment Company Act of 1940 to achieve several key objectives. This structure provides regulatory clarity under the SEC's new dealer rule while leveraging fixed capitalization to maintain portfolio stability. The closed-end format allows for efficient management of the municipal bond portfolio, reducing cash drag and operating expenses. Most importantly, the tender mechanism enables controlled liquidity for a permissionless, rebasing stablecoin while maintaining traditional market integration through the token-as-share-class model.

2 XFT MUNICIPAL INCOME FUND

XFT Municipal Income Fund is a continuously offered, diversified, closed-end management investment company registered under the Investment Company Act of 1940 (“1940 Act”). The Fund intends to operate as an “tender fund,” making repurchase offers for 5% to 25% of its outstanding shares at net asset value (“NAV”) on a discretionary basis, unless postponed or suspended under regulatory guidelines.

3 CLOSED-END FUNDS

A closed-end fund is an investment vehicle with a fixed number of shares that trades on an exchange at prices that can differ from its net asset value, allowing investors to potentially buy assets at a discount.

 

3.1 WHY CLOSED-END FUNDS?

1 Oldest US fund structure – 1893

2 Best known for income, international and municipal bonds

3 Discounts and premiums to NAV

4 Intraday trading (control)

5 Stop loss and limit orders

6 Best way to capture market inefficiencies

7 Ability to use three types of leverage

8 A diversified and professionally managed pool of assets

 

3.2 WHY CLOSED-END FUNDS FOR YIELD?

2 Leverage

3.4 THE DISCOUNT

CEFs are best known for their ‘infamous’ discount. While some investors and members of the financial press have yet to gain this understanding, the discount is the feature built into a CEF that makes them unique and potentially quite lucrative.

 

CEF Discounts, an example: If a CEF has a NAV of $10

per share and a market price of $8 per share, there is a

20% discount. If the NAV increases to $12 and the

market price increases to $11, then the discount is

reduced to 8.33%. The NAV has increased 20%, and the

investor has realized a market price gain of 37.5%.

 

3.5 NET ASSET-VALUE

NAV = (Market Value of All Securities Held by Fund + Cash and Equivalent Holdings – Fund Liabilities) ÷ Total Fund Shares Outstanding

 

4 TOKEN-AS-A-SHARE-CLASS

XFT implements a hub-and-spoke structure where the core municipal bond fund (hub) offers multiple share classes (spokes), including traditional mutual fund shares and two digital token variants: USDX, a rebasing stablecoin that maintains a $1 price while distributing yield daily, and USDXW, an accumulating token that tracks the fund's NAV.

The hub-and-spoke structure follows Vanguard's proven multi-class mutual fund model, which has saved investors over $1 trillion through reduced fees and operational efficiencies. With Vanguard's tax-efficiency patent expiring, XFT is positioned to modernize this approach for digital assets. Instead of issuing standalone stablecoins, XFT establishes a regulated investment fund that can issue multiple share classes - including traditional mutual fund shares and digital tokens (USDX and USDXW). This approach enables fund-level scale, tax efficiencies, and seamless interoperability between traditional and digital markets.
